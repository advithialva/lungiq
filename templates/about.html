About LungIQ



LungIQ is a deep learning-based diagnostic support tool designed to assist healthcare professionals in the early detection of lung diseases like pneumonia and COVID-19. By analyzing chest X-rays, LungIQ provides a quick preliminary diagnosis to help prioritize cases, especially in regions with limited access to radiologists.

Our Mission

Our mission is to bridge the gap in diagnostic healthcare by providing a reliable, accessible, and fast diagnostic support tool. LungIQ strives to assist in the early detection of life-threatening respiratory conditions, ensuring that healthcare resources are optimally allocated to the patients who need them most.

How It Works

LungIQ utilizes a convolutional neural network (CNN) model, trained on a large dataset of chest X-ray images. Users can upload a chest X-ray to LungIQ to receive an AI-generated assessment of potential lung abnormalities. The model provides a preliminary diagnosis along with a confidence score, assisting medical professionals in making informed decisions.

Why LungIQ?

  • High accuracy in detecting pneumonia and COVID-19 signs in X-rays.
  • Quick, accessible diagnostic support, reducing wait times.
  • Designed to assist overburdened healthcare systems by supporting early diagnosis in regions with limited resources.
  • Empowers radiologists and healthcare providers to make data-driven decisions.

Understanding Lung Conditions

Normal Lungs

Healthy lungs allow for efficient gas exchange, providing oxygen to the body and removing carbon dioxide. X-rays of normal lungs show clear air spaces and uniform tissue without any signs of infection or damage.


Maintaining Good Lung Health:

  • Avoid smoking and second-hand smoke.
  • Stay away from pollutants and chemicals.
  • Exercise regularly to improve lung capacity.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep airways clear.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in antioxidants.
  • Manage conditions like asthma with proper medication.

Pneumonia

Pneumonia is an infection of the lungs that causes inflammation. It can be caused by various factors, such as bacteria, viruses, and fungi. Early detection is key to effective treatment. The symptoms include a cough, fever, difficulty breathing, and chest pain.


Progression: If left untreated, pneumonia can worsen, leading to respiratory failure or septicemia. Early intervention with antibiotics or antivirals significantly improves recovery.


Treatment: Treatment usually involves antibiotics for bacterial pneumonia and antivirals for viral pneumonia. Oxygen therapy or ventilation may be required for severe cases.

COVID-19

COVID-19 is a respiratory illness caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It primarily affects the lungs and can cause severe respiratory distress. Symptoms include fever, cough, shortness of breath, and fatigue.


Progression: The disease can range from mild symptoms to severe pneumonia, leading to acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS) and multi-organ failure. Hospitalization may be required for severe cases.


Treatment: There is no specific cure for COVID-19. Treatment focuses on symptom management, such as using antiviral medications, oxygen therapy, and, in severe cases, mechanical ventilation. Vaccines have been developed for prevention.
